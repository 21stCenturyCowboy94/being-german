Guten Tag my friends. My name is N. Kenneth Ho, I am an American of Chinese, Irish, German, and Swedish descent. In this website, I will be exploring with you what life is like when you have ancestry from Germany, and the positives and negatives about it.


In the United States, people of German ancestry make up the second largest in the entire population, second only to English-descendants. In the 1800s and 1900s, more than seven-million Germans immigrated to the USA from Germany. The Germans immigrated to the USA for a number of reasons including war, poverty, religious persecution, political problems, economic insecurity, famine, crime, and other issues. Today the vast majority of German-Americans live in the Northern half of the United States, in a chunk of the country that goes from Pennsylvania to Oregon known as the "German Belt."


Today, not many Germans come to the USA, and Germany itself is now a country where many people immigrate to. Despite the fact a century has gone by since the waves of German immigrants came to the USA, the legacy of ther German immigration is still felt in many ways to this day.


In my home state of Oregon, about one-fifth of the population has German ancestry, which makes sense considering Oregon is in the German belt and that my DNA test showed that I am part-German.


Being German-American comes with a lot of things, includng positives like history, culture, food, and famous people, and negative things like bad history.


To start, the history of Germany is really cool. Germany has been the home of many things including the development of technology, science, and music. Many famous things were invented in Germany. Things like the automobile, the printing press, the cable train car, and blimp were all invented in Germany.


Many famous scientists and inventors came from Germany, including Werner von Siemens, Max Planck, Johannes Gutenberg, and Albert Einstein.


This website will explore the history and culture of Germany, and the realities and culture of German-Americans, including what its like, and the experiences we have.
